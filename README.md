## Hi, I'm Eleonora 👋
I'm a PhD student in Immunology at Sapienza University of Rome, with a background that spans tissue engineering, immunology, and computational biology.
My research path started with a three-year internship focused on the biocompatibility of engineered tissues, which gave me a solid foundation in experimental biology and cell behaviour in biomaterial contexts. During my Master's, I joined an immunology lab where I was mentored by the group's bioinformatician and developed hands-on expertise in the computational analysis of high-dimensional single-cell data.
I'm particularly interested in understanding how the immune microenvironment shapes disease — from tumour biology to tissue remodelling — and in building reproducible, well-documented analysis pipelines that connect raw sequencing data to biological insight.
